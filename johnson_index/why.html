What We Measure Affects Our Behavior (Joseph Stiglitz).

  • If you judge the performance of a researcher by the number of publications, you will get loads of publications. Scientists are usually smart people who know how to work the system.
  • Does it really improve the situation if individuals not are judged by these numbers, but the performance of the research institutes instead? It definitely seems to be a good idea to put the management department in charge of these numbers.
  • The real question is do we really want loads of publications, if only the numbers count?
  • Lines of Code as a performance indicator for software developers was abandoned a long time ago - because what you actually want is the simplest, most readable solution with the least lines of codes that is easy to maintain
